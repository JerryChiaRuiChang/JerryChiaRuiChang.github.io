Jerry (Chia-Rui) Chang

PhD Student in Biostatistics, Harvard University,

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Biostatistics at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. You’ll find a little about me on this page. Please use the tabs at the top of the page for more information on my projects, CV, and music.

I am originally from Taipei, Taiwan. I grew up playing the piano and participated in competitions both internationally and locally. Though my plan was to go to music conservatory for college, I decided to explore options outside of music. I have always found some interesting connections between music and mathematics, where technical training in music led me to think logically and quantitatively. I ended up studying Statistical Science with a minor in Computer Science at Duke University.

At Duke, I had the opportunities to work with amazing faculties in the Duke Statistical Science department, including Dr. Jerome Reiter, Dr. Fan Li, and Dr. Cynthia Rudin. These experiences sparked my interest in the intersection of statistics and public health, which led me to graduate schools and to biostatistics.

Currently, I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Rui Wang, where my research aims to develop novel statistical methods to address challenges in the design and analysis of clinical trials. In particular, my research concerns with (1) improving validity — the ability to produce statistically credible results and infer cause-effect relationships — of clinical trials in the face of complex trial designs and missing data challenges and (2) enhancing external validity — the extent to which trial results can be generalized/transported to some target population of interests, which may systematically differ from the original trial population due to inclusion/exclusion criteria.

Outside of school, I continue playing the piano. You can find my performance in the music tab. I am also a big foodie and enjoy cooking and trying out restaurants during my spare time.

Here’s short bio for my music performance.

Jerry Chia-Rui Chang is a PhD Student in the Department of Biostatistics at Harvard Chan School Public Health. Prior to coming to Harvard he studied piano at Duke with Prof. Pei-Fen Liu and Prof. Chiao-Han Liao in Taiwan. He has won awards in both international and national competitions. He was the third prize winner for 2010 Taipei International Chopin Piano Competition, a Finalist in the 2008 Gina Bachauer International Junior and Young Artist Piano Competitions in Salt Lake City, and a three-time National Champion in the National Taiwan Piano Competition. Since age of 12, Chia-Rui performed piano concerto with National Taiwan Symphony Orchestra including Mozart Piano Concerto No.23, Chopin Piano Concerto No.1, and Beethoven Piano Concerto No. 1. In 2009, he presented a solo piano recital in Taiwan’s National Concert Hall, sponsored by Steinway & Sons, New Art Inc. While at Duke, he was two time winner for the Duke concerto competition, where he performed Chopin Piano Concerto No.2 in his Freshman year and Liszt Piano Concerto No. 1 in his senior year.

I was fortunate to be interviewed by the Duke Chronicle about my performance of Liszt Piano Concerto No. 1 with the Duke Symphony Orchestra.
